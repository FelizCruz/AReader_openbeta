ArcReader's quiet superpower: it works without the internet.

Every chapter you read is saved on your device automatically. Want to stock up? Open any novel and download chapters in batches — fifty, two hundred, the whole book — then board the plane. Your library comes with you.

<img src="https://cdn.arcreaders.app/welcome_novel/img/offline_sync.png">

Two tips: set downloads to Wi-Fi only in Settings so they never eat your data, and pull down on your library screen to check for new chapters.

One last thing, worth thirty seconds: you're reading as a guest. Sign in — Settings, then Account, it's free — and your library, reading positions, highlights, and bookmarks follow you to every device.

That's the tour. "Display" to make reading comfortable, "+" to add books, headphones to listen, downloads for offline, sign in to keep it all safe.

Delete this guide whenever you like — press and hold it in your library. It was designed to be outgrown.

Happy reading.

— The ArcReader Team
